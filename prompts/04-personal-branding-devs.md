# Prompt: Personal Branding for Developer-Builders

## Context

I'm a developer who builds across multiple domains (Rust, Swift, TypeScript/Svelte, Python) and participates in hackathons regularly. I want to establish a recognizable personal brand as a builder/maker in the developer community. I'm not looking for influencer-style branding — I want to be known for the quality and creativity of what I build.

## Research Questions

1. **Developer brand archetypes**: What types of personal brands work in the developer space?
   - The Prolific Builder (ships constantly)
   - The Deep Expert (known for one domain)
   - The Educator (teaches what they learn)
   - The Explorer (tries everything, shares journey)
   - Which archetype fits a multi-stack hackathon participant?

2. **Portfolio as brand**: How to structure a portfolio/presence that shows range without looking scattered?
   - How to present diverse projects under a coherent narrative
   - GitHub profile optimization
   - Personal site structure for builder-types

3. **Hackathon presence as branding**: How to use hackathon participation strategically?
   - Documenting the process (before/during/after)
   - Networking at events
   - Turning wins (and losses) into content
   - Building reputation with organizers and sponsors

4. **Technical credibility signals**: What signals matter in the developer community?
   - Open source contributions vs. personal projects
   - Blog posts vs. talks vs. videos
   - Quality vs. quantity tradeoff
   - How to showcase AI/agent work specifically

5. **Brand consistency across platforms**: How to maintain a coherent developer brand across:
   - GitHub
   - Twitter/X
   - LinkedIn
   - YouTube / video content
   - What should be the same vs. different per platform?

## What I Want Back

- A personal brand positioning exercise I can do in 30 minutes
- Concrete checklist for GitHub profile, social bios, and portfolio
- Examples of developers with strong builder brands
- 90-day action plan for establishing presence
