# Prompt: Content Creation Strategy for Developers

## Context

I'm a developer who wants to build a content creation practice around my technical work — hackathon demos, project showcases, tutorials, and dev logs. I want a sustainable system, not a one-off effort. I work primarily with Rust, Swift, TypeScript/Svelte, and Python. I'm interested in AI agents, developer tools, and automation.

## Research Questions

1. **Content repurposing pipeline**: How can I turn one hackathon project into multiple content pieces?
   - Demo video → short clips for social
   - README → blog post / thread
   - Architecture decisions → technical article
   - Build process → dev log / timelapse
   - What tools automate this repurposing?

2. **Platform strategy for developers**: Where should developer content live for maximum reach?
   - YouTube vs. Twitter/X vs. LinkedIn vs. Dev.to vs. personal blog
   - What format works best on each platform?
   - How do algorithms favor developer content on each?

3. **Consistent production workflow**: What does a minimal, repeatable content workflow look like?
   - Record → edit → publish → distribute
   - How to batch content creation
   - Templates and checklists to reduce friction
   - How to keep it under 2 hours per piece

4. **Developer audience growth**: What actually works for growing a developer audience in 2025-2026?
   - Building in public patterns
   - Hackathon content as growth strategy
   - Technical depth vs. accessibility tradeoff
   - Community engagement vs. broadcast content

5. **Tools & automation**: What tools/services help developers create content faster?
   - AI writing assistants for technical content
   - Thumbnail generators
   - Social media schedulers for dev content
   - Analytics that matter for developer creators

## What I Want Back

- A concrete weekly/monthly content calendar template
- Toolchain recommendation (free/cheap, dev-friendly)
- Examples of developers who do this well and what to learn from them
